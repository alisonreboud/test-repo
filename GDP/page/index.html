ICT patents, GDP and Digital skills in Europe

By Alison Reboud and Thomas Schleider

Introduction

Are European countries equally benefiting from the Digital Economy ?

Information and communications technology (ICT) is more relevant than ever today.
By taking a look at patent application we chose one of several possible indicators of a country's performance in research.
We were mostly curious if there were correlations or interactions with the following points:

1. The GDP (nominal) per capita of a country

2. The percentage of the ICT sector of the GDP

3. The average level of digital skills of the popluation in form of advanced internet skills

-We used data from Eurostat from 2010-

Conclusion

The histograms show that the distribution of patents is less uniform than the distributions of GDP and skills, with Sweden and Finland having considereably higher results. As a general rule it seems that the more familiar a population is with digital devices the higher the number of patents applications. ICT represent between 2 and 6 percents of European countries GDP. Norway, Austria and the UK have a quite low number of patent applications in comparison to their rather high GDP.
